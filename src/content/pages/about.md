Product manager and startup operator. I have built and run fintech and B2B SaaS products end to end, from the customer problem to shipped software to the next iteration.

I co-founded and ran product at Talisman, a venture-backed platform that helps businesses and the accounting firms that serve them see and manage their spend, subscriptions, and transactions. As co-founder, I owned the product lifecycle: customer discovery, the roadmap, the specs, the releases, and the quality bar. We raised over $1M and grew to $113K in ARR. Before Talisman I worked in pricing, project and account management, and master data at McKesson, which is where I learned how financial data behaves at scale. I am looking for a product role where I can understand and empathize with users to own a problem end to end.

## What I care about

I got into product because I love the moment a real user problem turns into something people actually use. Empathy is the part I care about most. Without it, you can build something clever that nobody needs.

The decisions I am proudest of came from listening instead of defending a plan. Narrowing Talisman from a broad platform down to spend, and then following accountants into a market we had not planned for, both came from taking real user signal seriously over our original vision. I try to stay genuinely curious, hold my own ideas loosely, and keep the team organized enough to move fast with confidence.

This site is itself a work sample. I built it the way I build products: I researched the problem, wrote a PR/FAQ to align on the users and the why, specified it in a PRD with measurable targets, then shipped it with Claude Code. All of that is public in the repository.
